Problem 1

Problem 1.1

The decision variables (alongside their notation) include:

  1. The MW capacity installed for geothermal, coal, natural gas combined cycle gas turbine, natural gas combustion turbine, wind, and solar ($X_g$, $X_{coal}$, $X_{CCGT}$, $X_{CT}$, $X_{w}$, and $X_{s}$ respectively).

  2. The MW generated per type (geothermal, coal, CCGT, CT, wind, solar) for a given hour of the day t ($Y_{g,t}$, $Y_{coal,t}$, $Y_{CCGT,t}$, $Y_{CT,t}$, $Y_{w,t}$, and $Y_{s,t}$ respectively).

  3. Non served energy for a given hour of the day t ($NSE_t$).

Problem 1.2

The objective function to minimize will be the cost per year, defined as follows: Z=total investment cost + total operating cost + NSE cost, where total investment cost= $457000X_g+268000X_{coal}+85000X_{CCGT}+62580X_{CT}+92000X_{w}+92000X_{s}$, total operating cost = $22*365\sum_{t=0}^{24} Y_{coal,t} + 35*365\sum_{t=0}^{24} Y_{CCGT,t} + 45*365\sum_{t=0}^{24} Y_{CT,t}$, and NSE cost = $1000*365\sum_{t=0}^{24} NSE_t$.

The total investment cost is gotten by summing up the products of the investment cost for each type and the capacity installed for each type. The total operating cost was gotten by first summing up the MWh generated for each throughout the day for a given type. Each of these sums were multiplied by the operating cost for each type, and also by the factor $365 days / 1 year$ to result in annual costs. Since the operating cost for renewables is 0, only coal, CCGT, and CT were included. Lastly, the NSE cost was gotten from the penalty for non served energy multiplied by the total NSE for the year and added to the operating costs.

Problem 1.3

The constraints are as follows:

  1. Capacity constraints: for each hour of the day t, $Y_{g,t}\leq 0.95*X_g$, $Y_{coal,t}\leq X_{coal}$, $Y_{CCGT,t}\leq X_{CCGT}$, $Y_{CT,t}\leq X_{CT}$, $Y_{w,t}\leq CF_{w,t}*X_{w}$, and $Y_{s,t}\leq CF_{s,t}*X_{s}$,

where $CF_{w,t}$ and $CF_{s,t}$ are the capacity factors at a given hour of the day t for wind and solar respectively. This means that the MW generated cannot exceed the capacity of that type given its capacity factor.

  1. Demand constraints: $Y_{g,t} + Y_{coal,t} + Y_{CCGT,t} + Y_{CT,t} + Y_{s,t} + Y_{w,t} + NSE_t = D_t$ where $D_t$ is the demand for a given hour of the day t. This means the combined MW generated from each type (plus any non-served energy) has to meet the demand for each hour of the day.

  2. Non-negativity constraints: For a given generator type G, $X_G \geq 0$, and $Y_{G,t} \geq 0$. Also, $NSE_t \geq 0$. This is to prevent the solution from including negative capacity or power generated, which is physically impossible.

This set of constraints is sufficient to fully define the problem, as they account for and bound every single decision variable.
